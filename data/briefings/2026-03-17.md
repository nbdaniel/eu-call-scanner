**Score: 82 — Excellent Match**
[→ Official Call Page](https://ec.europa.eu/info/funding-tenders/opportunities/portal/screen/opportunities/topic-details/ERASMUS-YOUTH-2026-PCOOP-ENGO)

**What it is:** The 2026 edition of the KA2 Cooperation Partnerships call reserved exclusively for European NGOs active in the youth field. This is AMPE's home territory — transnational, NGO-led, youth-focused partnerships producing tangible outputs over 12–36 months.

**Why it fits AMPE:** Direct thematic match across all four of AMPE's core areas. The ENGO-specific strand removes competition from universities and public bodies. Romania is fully eligible and Romanian organisations historically perform well as coordinators in youth KA2 calls. AMPE's existing European networks mean partnership formation should be achievable within weeks, not months.

**Deadline:** ⚠️ Not yet published — confirm immediately via the EC Funding & Tenders Portal and with ANPCDEFP (Romanian National Agency).

**Budget:** ⚠️ Not yet published. Based on 2024/2025 precedent, expect €100,000–€400,000 per project. Well within AMPE's stated range.

**Key Requirements:**
- Minimum partnership threshold to confirm (typically 3 organisations from ≥3 Erasmus+ countries)
- Lead applicant must be a European-level or nationally active NGO
- At least one partner from a country other than the coordinator's
- Joint outputs and measurable impact on youth work practice required

**Next Steps:**
1. Check the EC portal daily for deadline publication — 2025 edition closed in April; expect similar for 2026
2. Identify 2–3 partner organisations from existing AMPE network and issue informal expression-of-interest letters this week
3. Begin scoping the project concept — pick one focused theme (e.g., democratic participation of youth, digital citizenship, youth in rural areas) rather than a broad umbrella
4. Contact ANPCDEFP to confirm whether the Romanian NA has any co-funding or pre-application support available
