### 🟢 Dezvoltarea unei rețele pentru tineret — Servicii personalizate pentru tineri NEET, ITI Delta Dunării
**Programme:** ESF+ | **Score:** 82 — Excellent Match | **Recommendation: Apply**

**What it is:** A competitive ESF+ call under the Youth Employment Operational Programme (PEO) targeting the ITI Delta Dunării territory. The project must establish or develop a youth network delivering personalised, quality services to young people, with a specific focus on NEETs (Not in Education, Employment, or Training).

**Why it fits AMPE:** AMPE's experience in youth services, non-formal education, and ESF+ project management makes this a strong fit thematically. NGOs are explicitly eligible. Romania-based, ESF+ co-financed projects are within AMPE's demonstrated implementation capacity. Note: the geographic focus is the Danube Delta ITI territory — AMPE must either demonstrate operational presence there or build a credible partnership with a locally anchored organisation.

**Deadline:** 26 May 2026 — **9.8 weeks from today. Act now.**

**Budget:** Not specified in the call data — verify the funding guide at [fonduri-structurale.ro](https://www.fonduri-structurale.ro/finantari/2094/dezvoltarea-unei-retele-pentru-tineret-care-sa-furnizeze-servicii-personalizate-si-de-calitate-tinerilor-cu-precadere-din-categoria-nee-ts-teritoriul-iti-delta-dunarii). ESF+ Standard Projects in Romania under PEO typically carry substantial budgets; co-financing requirements (typically 2% for NGOs under ESF+) must be confirmed.

**Key Requirements:**
- Project must target NEET youth in the ITI Delta Dunării territory specifically
- Demonstrate capacity or local partnership to deliver services in that territory
- Personalised service delivery methodology must be clearly defined
- ESF+ co-financing obligations apply — confirm percentage and source

**Next Steps:**
1. Download the full Ghid al Solicitantului from fonduri-structurale.ro this week and map eligibility criteria against AMPE's current capacity.
2. Identify and contact a partner organisation with established presence in the Danube Delta ITI territory (e.g., a local youth centre, AJOFM branch, or community organisation) — this is the single most critical action for this call.
3. Clarify co-financing requirements and confirm AMPE can meet them before committing to the application.
4. Begin structuring the NEET targeting and personalised services methodology — this will be a key evaluation criterion.
